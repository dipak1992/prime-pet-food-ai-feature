# Yak Chews vs Bully Sticks

Yak chews and bully sticks are both popular dog chews, but they solve different problems. Bully sticks are softer and usually easier to consume. Yak chews are denser and usually better when the goal is longer chew time, lower odor, and a cleaner indoor routine.

If your dog finishes treats quickly or needs more enrichment, the comparison matters before you buy.

## Chew duration

For many dogs, Himalayan yak cheese chews last longer than bully sticks because they are harder and lower moisture. A bully stick may be useful for a softer edible chew, but power chewers often finish them quickly. Yak chews usually create a slower chewing session.

Estimate your own dog's result here: [How long will this last for my dog?](/pages/how-long-will-this-last-for-my-dog)

## Odor and mess

Bully sticks can have a stronger smell and may leave more residue. Yak chews are typically lower odor and cleaner indoors. That makes them easier to use during work-from-home hours, evening calm time, or supervised crate practice.

## Ingredient preference

Prime Pet Food yak chews are made for owners who want simple ingredients and a dense chewing experience. If your priority is a quick edible chew, a bully stick may fit. If your priority is long-lasting enrichment, a yak chew is often the better place to start.

## Safety and sizing

Both chew types require supervision. With yak chews, choose a chew larger than your dog can swallow and remove it when it becomes a small nub. With bully sticks, watch for gulping behavior and fast consumption.
